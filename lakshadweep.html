Lakshadweep

Capital: Kavaratti

Population: ~65,000 (2023 est.)

Official Languages: Malayalam, Jeseri, Mahl, English

History: Lakshadweep is India's smallest Union Territory, consisting of 36 islands in the Arabian Sea. Only 10 are inhabited. Known for white sandy beaches, coral atolls, and blue lagoons, it is a pristine and eco-sensitive region. Access is limited, and a permit is required to visit.

Kavaratti Island

Location: Capital Island

Famous for: Marine aquarium, mosques, lagoon views

How to reach: Ship from Kochi or flight to Agatti + boat

Agatti Island

Location: Western Lakshadweep

Famous for: Airport, snorkeling, lagoons

How to reach: Only airport in Lakshadweep (from Kochi)

Minicoy Island

Location: Southernmost island

Famous for: Lighthouse, tuna fishing, Mahl culture

How to reach: Ship from Kochi (14-16 hrs)

Bangaram Island

Location: Near Agatti

Famous for: Dive resort, lagoons, bioluminescence

How to reach: Boat from Agatti Island

Kadmat Island

Location: North of Kavaratti

Famous for: Water sports, coral reefs

How to reach: Ship from Kochi or boat from Agatti

Kalpeni Island

Location: Central Lakshadweep

Famous for: Scenic lagoon, kayaking, coral debris

How to reach: Ship from Kochi (overnight)

Minicoy Lighthouse Beach

Location: Minicoy Island

Famous for: Tall lighthouse, panoramic view

How to reach: Accessible while exploring Minicoy

Suheli Par Atoll

Location: Uninhabited atoll southwest of Kavaratti

Famous for: Biodiversity, untouched coral life

How to reach: Special boat trips (with permit)

Marine Museum

Location: Kavaratti

Famous for: Coral specimens, marine awareness

How to reach: Walkable from Kavaratti port

Thinnakara Island

Location: Near Bangaram

Famous for: Tented stays, peaceful lagoon

How to reach: Boat from Agatti
