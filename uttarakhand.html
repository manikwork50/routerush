Uttarakhand

Brief History

Uttarakhand, the 'Land of the Gods', is famed for its majestic Himalayan ranges and deeply spiritual heritage. It boasts of ancient pilgrimage sites and serene hill stations integral to Hindu mythology and culture.

Major Holy Places & Attractions

  • Haridwar

    An ancient city situated on the banks of the Ganges, renowned for the Kumbh Mela, Ganga Aarti at Har Ki Pauri, and sacred ghats.
    How to reach: Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun - approx. 35 km); Haridwar railway station.

  • Rishikesh

    The 'Yoga Capital of the World', ideal for river rafting and spiritual retreats.
    How to reach: Jolly Grant Airport (Dehradun - approx. 20 km); Rishikesh railway station.

  • Dehradun

    Capital city with scenic surrounds, historic Robber's Cave, and forest reserves.
    How to reach: Jolly Grant Airport; Dehradun railway station.

  • Kedarnath

    Famous for the Kedarnath Temple, one of the twelve Jyotirlingas devoted to Lord Shiva.
    How to reach: Airports: Jolly Grant (Dehradun), road and helicopter access to Gaurikund, then trek.

  • Badrinath

    Renowned for the Badrinath Temple dedicated to Lord Vishnu, part of Char Dham pilgrimage.
    How to reach: Airports: Jolly Grant or Dehradun; road access during summer months.

  • Mussoorie

    Popular hill station known as the 'Queen of Hills' with panoramic mountain views.
    How to reach: Jolly Grant Airport; road from Dehradun.

  • Nainital

    Scenic lake city, famous for boating, lakeside markets, and pleasant climate.
    How to reach: Pantnagar Airport; Kathgodam railway station; road access.

  • Jim Corbett National Park

    India's oldest national park, popular for wildlife safaris and rich biodiversity.
    How to reach: Pantnagar Airport; Ramnagar railway station.

Local Cuisine

Uttarakhand cuisine is simple and wholesome, featuring dishes like Aloo Ke Gutke, Kafuli, Chainsoo, and Bal Mithai, a popular local sweet.
