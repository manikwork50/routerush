Arunachal Pradesh

Brief History

Formerly known as NEFA, Arunachal Pradesh became a state in 1987. It is home to diverse tribes such as the Monpa, Adi, and Apatani and has deeply rooted Buddhist and animist traditions.

Major Places to Visit

  • Tawang

    Why visit: Largest monastery in Arunachal Pradesh.
    How to reach: Airport: Tezpur (Assam, ~320 km), Road via Bomdila.

  • Ziro Valley

    Why visit: Known for music festivals and Apatani culture.
    How to reach: Airport: Lilabari (Assam, ~120 km), Road from Itanagar.

  • Bomdila

    Why visit: Scenic town with monasteries.
    How to reach: Airport: Tezpur (~150 km); Road via NH13.

  • Namdapha National Park

    Why visit: Largest protected rainforest in India.
    How to reach: Airport: Dibrugarh (~160 km), Rail: Tinsukia.

Local Cuisine

Enjoy Thukpa (noodle soup), momos (dumplings), gyapa khazi (Monpa pulao), apong (traditional rice beer), and bamboo shoot pickle.
