Andhra Pradesh

Brief History

Known as the “Rice Bowl of India,” Andhra Pradesh has a history tied to the Satavahanas, Eastern Chalukyas, and Vijayanagara Empire. Amaravati was an ancient Buddhist learning center. The region has been a cultural crossroads blending Dravidian and Telugu traditions.

Major Places to Visit

  • Tirupati & Tirumala: Home to the world-famous Sri Venkateswara Temple, one of the richest pilgrimage centers globally.
  • Vishakhapatnam (Vizag): Beautiful beaches (RK Beach, Yarada Beach), Kailasagiri Hills, Araku Valley, Submarine Museum.
  • Amaravati: Historic Buddhist stupa ruins and planned new capital of Andhra Pradesh.
  • Lepakshi: Famous for the Veerabhadra Temple, mural paintings, and giant Nandi statue.

Local Cuisine

Must-try dishes include Tirupati Laddu (prasadam), Pulihora (tamarind rice), Bongu Chicken (bamboo chicken), Chepala Pulusu (fish curry), and Pootharekulu (paper-like sweet). Andhra Pradesh is famous for its flavorful, spicy dishes and unique pickles like Gongura.

Culture & Crafts

The state is known for Kondapalli toys, Kalamkari textiles (Machilipatnam and Srikalahasti styles), and Bidriware crafts. Festivals like Ugadi, Sankranti, and Brahmotsavam at Tirupati are major cultural highlights.
