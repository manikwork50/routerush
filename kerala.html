Kerala

Capital: Thiruvananthapuram

Population: ~35 million (2023 est.)

Official Language: Malayalam

History: Known as "God's Own Country", Kerala is famous for its palm-lined beaches, backwaters, spices, classical arts, Ayurveda, and literacy. It was formed in 1956 on linguistic grounds.

Munnar

Location: Idukki district

Famous for: Tea plantations, scenic hills

How to reach: Cochin Airport (110 km), Aluva Railway (100 km)

Alleppey (Alappuzha)

Location: Alappuzha district

Famous for: Backwaters, houseboat cruises

How to reach: Rail to Alappuzha, Cochin Airport (75 km)

Wayanad

Location: Wayanad district

Famous for: Forests, waterfalls, wildlife

How to reach: Road from Calicut (86 km); no direct rail

Thekkady

Location: Idukki district

Famous for: Periyar Tiger Reserve, lake cruise

How to reach: 150 km from Kochi by road

Kochi

Location: Ernakulam district

Famous for: Fort Kochi, Chinese fishing nets

How to reach: Cochin International Airport, Ernakulam Junction

Varkala

Location: Thiruvananthapuram

Famous for: Cliffside beach, clean waters

How to reach: Varkala Railway Station; 40 km from Trivandrum

Kovalam

Location: Near Thiruvananthapuram

Famous for: Lighthouse Beach, surfing

How to reach: 15 km from Trivandrum by road

Bekal Fort

Location: Kasaragod district

Famous for: Sea-facing historic fort

How to reach: Bekal Fort Railway Station nearby

Athirappilly Falls

Location: Thrissur district

Famous for: Largest waterfall in Kerala

How to reach: 55 km from Kochi by road

Silent Valley

Location: Palakkad district

Famous for: Evergreen rainforests, rare wildlife

How to reach: Road from Mannarkkad (40 km)
