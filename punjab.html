Punjab

Capital: Chandigarh

Population: ~30 million (2023 est.)

Official Language: Punjabi

History: Punjab, meaning "Land of Five Rivers", is known for its rich Sikh heritage, vibrant culture, fertile fields, and historic landmarks. It's a key agricultural hub and the birthplace of the Sikh faith.

Golden Temple

Location: Amritsar

Famous for: Holiest Sikh shrine with a gold-covered sanctum

How to reach: Amritsar Airport or Amritsar Railway Station

Jallianwala Bagh

Location: Amritsar

Famous for: Memorial to 1919 massacre

How to reach: Near Golden Temple, same city access

Wagah Border

Location: Near Amritsar

Famous for: India-Pakistan daily retreat parade

How to reach: 30 km from Amritsar by taxi/bus

Anandpur Sahib

Location: Rupnagar district

Famous for: Birthplace of Khalsa, major Sikh pilgrimage

How to reach: Nangal Railway Station nearby

Qila Mubarak

Location: Patiala

Famous for: 18th-century royal fort and palace

How to reach: Patiala Railway Station, by road

Virasat-e-Khalsa

Location: Anandpur Sahib

Famous for: Sikh heritage museum

How to reach: 80 km from Chandigarh by road

Ludhiana Museum

Location: Ludhiana

Famous for: Culture, history, and folk art of Punjab

How to reach: Ludhiana Railway Station

Ropar Wetland

Location: Rupnagar

Famous for: Birdwatching, biodiversity

How to reach: Road/train to Ropar (Rupnagar)

Bathinda Fort

Location: Bathinda

Famous for: Historic 1900-year-old fort

How to reach: Bathinda Railway Station

Harike Wetland

Location: Tarn Taran district

Famous for: Migratory birds, nature reserve

How to reach: 60 km from Amritsar by road
