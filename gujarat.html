Gujarat

Capital: Gandhinagar

Population: ~70 million (2023 est.)

Official Language: Gujarati

History: Gujarat, known for its vibrant culture, ancient Indus Valley sites, and industrial development, was home to Mahatma Gandhi. It boasts incredible temples, wildlife, and unique salt desert landscapes like the Rann of Kutch.

Rann of Kutch

Location: Kutch District

Famous for: Salt desert, Rann Utsav festival

How to reach: Bhuj (80 km); well connected by train/air

Gir National Park

Location: Junagadh District

Famous for: Asiatic lions, wildlife safaris

How to reach: Nearest rail: Veraval (42 km); road from Junagadh

Somnath Temple

Location: Prabhas Patan, Gir Somnath

Famous for: One of 12 Jyotirlingas of Shiva

How to reach: Veraval Railway Station (7 km)

Statue of Unity

Location: Kevadiya, Narmada District

Famous for: World's tallest statue (Sardar Patel)

How to reach: Nearest city: Vadodara (90 km); by train/bus

Dwarkadhish Temple

Location: Dwarka

Famous for: One of the Char Dham pilgrimage sites

How to reach: Dwarka Railway Station; well connected

Saputara

Location: Dang District

Famous for: Gujarat's only hill station, lake & gardens

How to reach: Nearest rail: Waghai (50 km); road from Surat (160 km)

Lothal

Location: Ahmedabad District

Famous for: Indus Valley ruins, dockyard

How to reach: 78 km from Ahmedabad via road

Champaner-Pavagadh

Location: Panchmahal District

Famous for: UNESCO World Heritage city ruins

How to reach: 50 km from Vadodara by road

Rani ki Vav

Location: Patan

Famous for: Stepwell architecture, UNESCO heritage site

How to reach: 125 km from Ahmedabad by road/train

Girnar Hills

Location: Junagadh

Famous for: Jain temples, trekking, ropeway

How to reach: Junagadh city by train/road
