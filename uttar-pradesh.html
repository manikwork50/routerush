Uttar Pradesh

Brief History

Uttar Pradesh is one of the oldest and most historically significant states in India. Known as the land of Lord Rama and Lord Krishna, it is the birthplace of two major Hindu deities and a major center of ancient Indian culture, religion, and history.

Major Holy Places & Attractions

  • Varanasi (Kashi)

    One of the world's oldest living cities, Varanasi is a sacred pilgrimage site on the banks of the Ganges. Famous for Kashi Vishwanath temple and the ghats.
    How to reach: Lal Bahadur Shastri Airport; Varanasi Junction railway station; well connected by road.

  • Ayodhya

    Birthplace of Lord Rama, with temples and the Ram Janmabhoomi complex.
    How to reach: Chaudhary Charan Singh Airport (Lucknow, 135 km); Ayodhya railway station; connected by road.

  • Mathura and Vrindavan

    Birthplace and playground of Lord Krishna, with numerous temples and festivals.
    How to reach: Agra Airport (60 km); Mathura Junction railway; well connected by road.

  • Taj Mahal, Agra

    UNESCO World Heritage and one of the Seven Wonders of the World.
    How to reach: Kheria Airport (Agra); Agra Cantt Platform; connected by road.

  • Sarnath

    Important Buddhist pilgrimage site where Buddha gave his first sermon.
    How to reach: Varanasi Airport; railway station and road access from Varanasi.

Other Prominent Attractions

  • Fatehpur Sikri - Historic Mughal city near Agra.
  • Allahabad (Prayagraj) - Triveni Sangam and Kumbh Mela site.
  • Lucknow - Capital city known for Nawabi heritage, cuisine, and architecture.
  • Kanpur, Noida, and Gorakhpur - Important urban centers with cultural significance.

Local Cuisine

Uttar Pradesh cuisine includes dishes like kebabs, biryanis, kachori, chaat (especially in Lucknow and Varanasi), Petha sweet from Agra, and rich Mughlai and Awadhi culinary traditions.
