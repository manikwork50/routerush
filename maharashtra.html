Maharashtra

Capital: Mumbai (Executive), Nagpur (Winter)

Population: ~125 million (2023 est.)

Official Language: Marathi

History: Maharashtra, the land of Marathas and Shivaji, is India's second most populous state known for its rich history, Bollywood, coastal beauty, and UNESCO heritage sites.

Mumbai

Location: Mumbai district

Famous for: Bollywood, Gateway of India, Marine Drive

How to reach: Chhatrapati Shivaji Airport, Mumbai CST Station

Ajanta Caves

Location: Aurangabad district

Famous for: Buddhist rock-cut caves and paintings

How to reach: Jalgaon/Aurangabad Railway Station

Pune

Location: Pune district

Famous for: Education hub, Shaniwar Wada, culture

How to reach: Pune Airport, Pune Railway Station

Mahabaleshwar

Location: Satara district

Famous for: Strawberry farms, viewpoints, hills

How to reach: Pune (120 km), Satara Railway Station

Lonavala

Location: Pune district

Famous for: Monsoon getaways, forts, waterfalls

How to reach: Lonavala Railway Station, road from Pune/Mumbai

Elephanta Caves

Location: Elephanta Island, Mumbai Harbour

Famous for: Hindu rock-cut cave temples

How to reach: Ferry from Gateway of India

Shirdi

Location: Ahmednagar district

Famous for: Sai Baba Temple pilgrimage

How to reach: Sainagar Shirdi Railway Station, Shirdi Airport

Tadoba National Park

Location: Chandrapur district

Famous for: Tiger reserve, wildlife safaris

How to reach: Chandrapur Railway Station, Nagpur (150 km)

Nashik

Location: Nashik district

Famous for: Vineyards, Kumbh Mela, temples

How to reach: Nashik Road Railway Station

Rajmachi Fort

Location: Near Lonavala

Famous for: Trekking and fort ruins

How to reach: Trek from Lonavala (16 km)
