Sikkim

Brief History

Sikkim, nestled in the Himalayas, is known for its rich Buddhist culture, scenic mountain beauty, and warm hospitality. It was a monarchy until 1975 and is now India’s smallest state by population.

Major Places to Visit

  • Gangtok

    Why visit: Capital city with panoramic Himalayan views, vibrant markets and monasteries.
    How to reach: Pakyong Airport (16 km); road connectivity with Siliguri.

  • Tsomgo Lake

    Why visit: High altitude glacial lake known for breathtaking blue waters.
    How to reach: 38 km from Gangtok by road via Nathula Pass.

  • Nathula Pass

    Why visit: Historic mountain pass on Indo-China border accessible seasonally.
    How to reach: 54 km from Gangtok, requires permit.

  • Yumthang Valley

    Why visit: Scenic valley known as the Valley of Flowers, beautiful year-round.
    How to reach: Around 150 km from Gangtok by road.

  • Rumtek Monastery

    Why visit: Important Tibetan Buddhist monastery with colorful ceremonies.
    How to reach: About 24 km from Gangtok.

Local Cuisine

Sikkimese cuisine blends Tibetan and Nepali influences offering dishes like Momos, Thukpa (noodle soup), Gundruk (fermented leafy greens), and locally brewed Chhaang (rice beer).
