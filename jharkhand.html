Jharkhand

Capital: Ranchi

Population: ~39 million (2023 est.)

Official Language: Hindi

History: Jharkhand was carved out of Bihar in 2000. Known for its forests, waterfalls, rich tribal culture, and mineral wealth, it is a blend of natural beauty and heritage.

Hundru Falls

Location: Ranchi District

Famous for: One of the highest waterfalls in Jharkhand

How to reach: 45 km from Ranchi; accessible by road

Dassam Falls

Location: Near Taimara village

Famous for: 10-stream waterfall, scenic spot

How to reach: 34 km from Ranchi on NH-33

Netarhat

Location: Latehar District

Famous for: Hill station, sunrise & sunset views

How to reach: 156 km from Ranchi by road

Betla National Park

Location: Palamu District

Famous for: Wildlife safari, elephants, tigers

How to reach: Daltonganj (25 km); connected by train/road

Patratu Valley

Location: Ramgarh District

Famous for: Scenic road curves and lake

How to reach: 40 km from Ranchi via road

Rajrappa Temple

Location: Ramgarh

Famous for: Chhinnamasta Temple and river confluence

How to reach: 65 km from Ranchi; buses/taxis available

Jagannath Temple

Location: Ranchi

Famous for: 17th-century temple similar to Puri

How to reach: 10 km from Ranchi city center

McCluskieganj

Location: Ranchi District

Famous for: Colonial-era Anglo-Indian town

How to reach: 65 km from Ranchi by road

Baidyanath Dham

Location: Deoghar

Famous for: One of the 12 Jyotirlingas of Lord Shiva

How to reach: Jasidih Railway Station (7 km)

Parasnath Hills

Location: Giridih District

Famous for: Jain pilgrimage site with temples

How to reach: Rail to Parasnath Station; trek to summit
