Dadra & Nagar Haveli and Daman & Diu

Capital: Daman

Population: ~6.2 lakh (2023 est.)

Official Languages: Gujarati, Hindi, Marathi, English

History: This Union Territory was formed in 2020 by merging two Portuguese-colonial regions: Daman and Diu, and Dadra and Nagar Haveli. These coastal towns and forested inland areas are known for Portuguese architecture, tranquil beaches, tribal culture, and heritage forts.

Devka Beach

Location: Daman

Famous for: Sunset walks, rocky shore

How to reach: Auto/taxi from Vapi Railway Station

Jampore Beach

Location: South Daman

Famous for: Black sand, horse rides

How to reach: 6 km from Daman city

Moti Daman Fort

Location: Daman

Famous for: Portuguese-era fort, lighthouse

How to reach: Local transport from Daman

St. Paul's Church

Location: Diu

Famous for: Baroque Portuguese architecture

How to reach: Walkable within Diu town

Diu Fort

Location: Diu Island

Famous for: Sea-facing fortress with lighthouse

How to reach: Near Diu Jetty

Ghoghla Beach

Location: Diu

Famous for: Blue flag beach, peaceful waters

How to reach: Diu Airport or bus stand

Dudhni Lake

Location: Dadra & Nagar Haveli

Famous for: Boating, forest surroundings

How to reach: 40 km from Silvassa by road

Lion Safari Wildlife Park

Location: Vasona, near Silvassa

Famous for: Asiatic lions, jeep safari

How to reach: 10 km from Silvassa city

Tribal Cultural Museum

Location: Silvassa

Famous for: Tribal artifacts, culture & heritage

How to reach: Central Silvassa

Satmaliya Deer Park

Location: Near Khanvel

Famous for: Spotted deer, watchtower view

How to reach: 12 km from Silvassa
