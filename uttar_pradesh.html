Uttar Pradesh

Capital: Lucknow

Population: ~24 crore (2023 est.)

Official Language: Hindi

History: Uttar Pradesh, the most populous state in India, holds immense historical, spiritual, and cultural importance. It is home to ancient cities like Varanasi and Mathura, Mughal architectural marvels in Agra, and the political nerve center of India. The Ganges River flows through it, nurturing spirituality and civilization alike.

Taj Mahal

Location: Agra

Famous for: Iconic white marble mausoleum

How to reach: Agra Cantt Station (6 km), Agra Airport (7 km), Bus available

Varanasi Ghats

Location: Varanasi

Famous for: Spiritual ceremonies, Ganga Aarti

How to reach: Varanasi Junction (3 km), Airport (25 km)

Fatehpur Sikri

Location: Near Agra (40 km)

Famous for: Red sandstone Mughal city, Buland Darwaza

How to reach: Train to Fatehpur Sikri station or bus from Agra

Sarnath

Location: Near Varanasi (10 km)

Famous for: Buddha's first sermon, Stupa

How to reach: Auto/rickshaw from Varanasi

Ayodhya

Location: Faizabad District

Famous for: Shri Ram Janmbhoomi temple, spiritual town

How to reach: Ayodhya Cantt or Faizabad Station, 10 km from each other

Bara Imambara

Location: Lucknow

Famous for: Labyrinth (Bhul Bhulaiya), Mughal architecture

How to reach: Auto from Lucknow Charbagh station (4 km)

Mathura

Location: Mathura City

Famous for: Birthplace of Lord Krishna

How to reach: Mathura Junction (3 km)

Vrindavan

Location: Near Mathura

Famous for: Temples like Prem Mandir, Banke Bihari

How to reach: 12 km from Mathura by auto/bus

Triveni Sangam

Location: Prayagraj (Allahabad)

Famous for: Confluence of Ganga, Yamuna, Saraswati

How to reach: Prayagraj Junction, auto to Sangam
