Chhattisgarh

Capital: Raipur

Population: ~30 million (2023 est.)

Official Language: Hindi, Chhattisgarhi

History: Carved out of Madhya Pradesh in 2000, Chhattisgarh is a mineral-rich state with dense forests, tribal culture, and scenic waterfalls. It holds archaeological and natural wonders, along with ancient temples and forts.

Chitrakote Falls

Location: Bastar District

Famous for: "Niagara of India", wide horseshoe-shaped fall

How to reach: Jagdalpur (40 km); Jagdalpur connected by rail & bus

Barnawapara Sanctuary

Location: Mahasamund District

Famous for: Leopards, bison, sloth bears, eco-tourism

How to reach: 100 km from Raipur by road

Bhoramdeo Temple

Location: Kabirdham District

Famous for: Khajuraho-style temple, 11th century architecture

How to reach: 135 km from Raipur via Kawardha

Tirathgarh Falls

Location: Kanger Valley National Park

Famous for: Tiered white waterfalls in lush forest

How to reach: 38 km from Jagdalpur by road

Kanger Valley National Park

Location: Bastar

Famous for: Limestone caves, wildlife, tribal forests

How to reach: Access from Jagdalpur by road

Danteshwari Temple

Location: Dantewada

Famous for: One of the 52 Shakti Peethas

How to reach: Nearest major town: Jagdalpur (80 km); by road

Rajim

Location: Raipur District

Famous for: Triveni Sangam, ancient temples, Rajim Kumbh

How to reach: 45 km from Raipur; buses and taxis available

Sirpur

Location: Mahasamund District

Famous for: Buddhist monasteries, archaeological ruins

How to reach: 78 km from Raipur via NH6

Malhar

Location: Bilaspur District

Famous for: Ancient temple ruins, Pataleshwar temple

How to reach: 40 km from Bilaspur; local buses available

Maitri Bagh

Location: Bhilai

Famous for: Zoo, musical fountain, Japanese-style garden

How to reach: Within Bhilai city; 30 km from Raipur
